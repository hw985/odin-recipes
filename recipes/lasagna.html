Lasagna

Description of the lasagna recipe.

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish renowned for its rich layers and comforting flavors. It consists of wide, flat sheets of pasta layered alternately with a savory mixture of ingredients such as seasoned ground meat (often beef or pork), tomato sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and a generous amount of melted mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. The dish is baked until the top is golden and bubbly, creating a delightful combination of textures—from the tender pasta to the hearty meat sauce and creamy cheese layers.

This versatile casserole is beloved worldwide for its ability to be customized with various fillings, including vegetables like spinach or mushrooms for vegetarian versions. Lasagna’s origins trace back to Italy, where it has been a staple of home cooking and festive meals for centuries. Its layered structure not only makes it visually appealing but also allows each bite to deliver a harmonious blend of flavors and a satisfying, hearty experience that epitomizes comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna pasta sheets (about 12 sheets)
  • Ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork) – 500 grams (about 1 lb)
  • Onion – 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Garlic – 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes – 700 ml (about 24 oz)
  • Tomato paste – 2 tablespoons
  • Olive oil – 2 tablespoons
  • Salt – to taste
  • Black pepper – to taste
  • Dried Italian herbs (such as oregano, basil, thyme) – 1-2 teaspoons
  • Ricotta cheese – 400 grams (about 14 oz)
  • Mozzarella cheese – 300 grams (about 10 oz), shredded
  • Parmesan cheese – 100 grams (about 3.5 oz), grated
  • Eggs – 1 or 2 (to mix with ricotta for creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional) – a handful
  • Milk – 500 ml (about 2 cups) (for béchamel sauce if used)
  • Butter – 50 grams (about 3.5 tablespoons) (for béchamel sauce)
  • All-purpose flour – 50 grams (about 3.5 tablespoons) (for béchamel sauce)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Cook the lasagna pasta sheets according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, sauté until soft and fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef (or beef and pork mix) to the pan. Cook until browned and fully cooked, breaking up the meat as it cooks.
  5. Stir in tomato paste, tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, dried Italian herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes until thickened.
  6. While the sauce simmers, prepare the béchamel sauce: melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat, whisk in flour and cook for 1-2 minutes. Gradually add milk while whisking continuously until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with eggs and a pinch of salt for creaminess.
  8. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
  9. Layer lasagna sheets over the sauce, then spread a layer of the ricotta mixture, followed by meat sauce, béchamel sauce, and a sprinkle of shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan.
  10. Repeat the layering process (pasta, ricotta, meat sauce, béchamel, cheeses) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a top layer of pasta covered with béchamel and cheese.
  11. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes.
  12. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  13. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving to allow it to set and make slicing easier.
  14. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired and serve warm.
